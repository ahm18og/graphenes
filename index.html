What are graphenes?

Graphenes are materials that are extracted from graphites and is made up of pure carbon. Graphines were founded in the year 2003 - 2004 by Andre Geim and Kostya Novoselov at the University of Manchester. Graphenes are atom thick, meaning their thickness is the same thickness as a single atom.

Graphenes are flexible and light while also being tough and has high-resistance, and it conducts electricity very well too meaning it can make for better technologies like bendable and lighter phones, and other devices with faster charging and better performance.

Graphenes are also great at handling heat, relating to how it's also good at electricity conduction, meaning that this is also important for electronic components that heats alot like computers or batteries.
